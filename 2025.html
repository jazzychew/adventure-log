Right at the end of fall semester, I was simply checking my emails when I saw there was an open research opportunity. I read the lab is in nuclear physics, but I decided to shoot my shot anyway.

I guess checking my email paid off since later during break, I had interviewed to join the group, and I scheduled my first on-campus meeting!

To explain my role, I would be in charge of creating a process to ensure the quality of light guides (mirrors). During the winter, I worked with a senior who had worked on the light guides before to learn the polishing process to create the mirrors as well as available resources we could use for the QA process.

It was a bit nerve-wracking to be a part of a lab since I had never experienced it before, but I'm glad I had Shreyas help me, and I hope to progress further as I grow more accustomed to the lab.
